Welcome to Jupiter!

The largest planet in our Solar System.

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and by far the largest — a gas giant with a mass more than twice that of all the other planets combined. Its diameter is 11 times that of Earth and a tenth that of the Sun. Jupiter orbits the Sun at a distance of 5.20 AU (778.5 Gm), with an orbital period of 11.86 years. It is the third-brightest natural object in the Earth's night sky, after the Moon and Venus, and has been observed since prehistoric times. Its name derives from that of Jupiter, the chief deity of ancient Roman religion.

(Wikipedia Contributors, 2019)

Fun facts about Jupiter!

Jupiter's most famous feature is the Great Red Spot, a giant storm larger than Earth that has been raging for at least 350 years.

It has at least 95 known moons, including the four large Galilean moons — Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto — discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610.

NASA's Juno spacecraft has been orbiting Jupiter since 2016, mapping its magnetic field and peering beneath its swirling cloud tops.
